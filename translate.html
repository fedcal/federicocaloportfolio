Modus Operandi

Confronting and knowing how to listen is perhaps one of the most important aspects of a developer's job. To create a website or application, a preliminary meeting is necessary to understand the user's needs, what are his goals, his budget, the functionalities of the application or website and to whom they are addressed. In fact, one of the first steps for the realization of the project is to make an accurate analysis, going into the smallest details without neglecting any aspect of the project. Then we will move on to a phase of realization of the project skeleton, at the end of which a report will be sent to the customer with a possible video call appointment in case you realize you need to make changes in some areas of the project. Afterwards, once the project is near the end of its embryonic state, has been validated for the most part and has a good structure, we meet again to discuss the style that the program's user interface or website should have. It all ends with the presentation and delivery of the project, attaching the documentation, the test cases carried out on it and an exhaustive report of the various processes and choices that led me to make certain design decisions.
